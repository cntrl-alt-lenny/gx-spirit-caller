## Reshape hints

- Declare `unsigned char b = p[i]` inside the outer loop body (not before it); the local scope keeps b promoted to a positive value, ensuring asr rather than lsl in the shift
- The `if ((b >> j) & 1) count++` idiom must stay as-is to produce `ands; addne`; splitting into a temp variable (`int bit = (b >> j) & 1; if (bit)`) may change the instruction pair
- If verify.py shows the outer shift origin (i=0, b used before j starts) in the wrong register, try hoisting the `unsigned char b` declaration to just before the inner loop instead of at the top of the outer loop body

- Run verify.py --cc all; reg-alloc flips appear as systematic register-number differences throughout the inner loop body
