## Carve-status correction (a wave-1 bug)

Wave 1 (brief 371) reported three TIER-3 entries as "already carved" and skipped
them: `ov010 021b46a8`, `ov010 021b2490`, `ov016 021b6074`. **That was wrong** ‚Äî
a false positive in the wave-1 carve-check, which grepped the bare address
*anywhere* in `delinks.txt`. A function's address routinely appears as the
*previous* function's `end:0x‚Ä¶`, so the check mis-flagged live funcs as carved.
**All three were actually live.**

The correct check is `start:0x<addr>` (each carved block's `start` is its own
address) ‚Äî or just run `asm_escape`, whose `gap_object()` is authoritative (a
carved func is no longer in the gap `.o`). This wave recovered 2 of the 3
(`ov010 021b2490`, `ov016 021b6074`). The third hit a tooling edge (below).

(Net effect on wave 1: it shipped 8 real funcs as claimed and the ROM is
byte-identical; the error was only in the *skip list* ‚Äî three live funcs were
deferred a wave, not anything mis-shipped.)

## Deferred ‚Äî `asm_escape` trailing-pool reloc attachment (`func_ov010_021b46a8`)

`021b46a8` (0xa8) failed `asm_escape`'s self-verify with a **spurious** diff: the
flagged word's bytes are identical
(`e8bd80f8(pop {‚Ä¶pc}) vs e8bd80f8(pop {‚Ä¶pc})`). The mismatch is a
reloc-*attachment* artifact in `parse_objdump`: a relocation that belongs to a
trailing literal-pool word gets attached to the preceding `pop` instead, because
objdump interleaves the `R_ARM_*` reloc line differently for the orig delinked
`.o` vs the freshly-assembled `.o`. This is the trailing-constant-pool bug brief
358 already noted. The function is almost certainly byte-exact (the *bytes*
match), but `asm_escape` can't confirm it, so it is **not** shipped. Proper fix:
associate relocs to words by address, not "most recent word". Spawned as a
follow-up tooling task.
