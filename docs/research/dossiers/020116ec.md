## Struct context

Near-clone of the **02011620** / **02011688** sincos family — same `data_020bef80` table, same `>>4` index — but the output layout is a Z-axis rotation (or similar) where `b` appears at both `[0]` and `[4]` (positions 0 and 0x10) and `-a` appears at `[3]` (position 0xc). The `0x1000` constant at `[8]` (offset 0x20) is the identity diagonal entry.

| offset | field | type | use |
|--------|-------|------|-----|
| data_020bef80[2n] | a | short (ldrsh) | first halfword; used at out[1], negated at out[3] |
| data_020bef80[2n+1] | b | short (ldrsh) | second halfword; reused at both out[0] and out[4] |

The critical constraint is that `b` must be held in a register across both `out[0]` and `out[4]` stores (no reload). The ASM uses `stmib r0, {r1,r2}` for the `out[1]=a; out[2]=0` pair, which requires `a` and 0 to already be in adjacent registers `r1/r2` at that point.
